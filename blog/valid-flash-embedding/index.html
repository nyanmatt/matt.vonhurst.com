XHTML 1.0 Transitional Valid Flash Embedding - Web Development

06 December 2009

It's incredible the things you take for granted. I recently helped a friend clean-up the code on his site. I don't generally use a whole lot of flash in the things I work on, but his page had a good amount of embedded movies. At first I just grabbed the code provided by youtube, viemo, etc. and plugged it in. Works fine; but then I went to validate:

Line XX, Column YY: element "embed" undefined. Did you mean "meta" or "em"?

Really? So, I did some digging. If you've ever looked into this problem, you've probably stumbled upon A List Apart's Flash Satay. Here is their solution in a nutshell:

<object
type="application/x-shockwave-flash" data="movie.swf"
width="xxx" height="yyy">
<param name="movie" value="movie.swf" />
</object>

There is more to their solution to solve a small problem in IE. That problem being, movies won't stream -- they will only play after completely loading. Not a big deal for small movies, so I left it out. Head over to the Flash Satay article for a more in-depth look!
