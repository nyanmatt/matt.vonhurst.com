Hidden OSX Features - Human-Computer Interaction (HCI)

26 January 2010

Like many software developers around my age, I've always used Windows. I've had a MacBook for about 7 months now, purchased against my will(or rather, against my wallet's will). Once I got accustomed to a new operating system, I started finding things I loved about it. Stupid things most OSX users already know, but things a lifetime Windows user would never consider.

The Spacebar. In Finder, the spacebar is like a super preview hotkey. It's like Right Click->Properties but at the slap of a thumb. Use it on folders, video, audio, pictures, documents, etc. My next goal is to find a way to tap into that preview feature and add some more functionalitiy.

Control + Mousewheel Up/Down. This hotkey provides a very smooth full-screen zoom. I hit this a couple times by accident before actually figuring out how to do it. Hold Control and scroll with your mousewheel or two-fingers on a multi-touch mousepad.

Command + Shift + 4 Or Command + $ if you like to simplify. This gives you a cross-hair to drag and screenshot a portion of your screen. Screenshots are saved on your desktop. Thanks Thomas Puleo for that tip!

I'm going to update this post as often as I find a new feature I didn't know about. If you've got an interesting feature you've stumbled upon in OSX, send me an e-mail (mathyu (at) gmail (dot) com) and I'll post it up!
